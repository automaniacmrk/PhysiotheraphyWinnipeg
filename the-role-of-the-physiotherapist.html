The Role of the Physiotherapist

A physiotherapist's role is diverse and not always the same over two days. The physiotherapist might need to evaluate a patient to determine if there are any problems. They may also need to help patients with walking, crutches or other mobility issues.

Education is also an essential part of physiotherapy. Physiotherapists spend a lot of time educating patients, their families and the community to prevent injuries and help people live a healthy lifestyle. A physiotherapist may also plan and implement community fitness programs. If necessary, physiotherapists can also issue sick leave certificates.

During their careers, physiotherapists treat all kinds of people, including children with cerebral palsy, premature babies, pregnant women, rehabilitation workers, athletes, the elderly (to try and get them fitter) and those who need assistance after heart disease, strokes, or major surgery.

Physical therapy has many benefits

Physical therapy can have many benefits depending on what the treatment is.

  • Pain management that reduces the need for opioids
  • avoiding surgery
  • improved mobility and movement
  • recovery from injury or trauma
  • Paralysis or stroke recovery
  • fall prevention
  • Balance improvement
  • management of age-related medical problems

A sports therapist can help an athlete maximize their performance by strengthening specific parts of the body and using muscles in new ways.

Individuals can be advised by a physical therapist from Beverly Physiotherapy, or another healthcare professional about their individual medical needs and the best treatment options.
