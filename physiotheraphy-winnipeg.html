What Should You Expect When You Visit A Physiotherapist At Beverly Physiotherapy?

Your injury could make it more difficult for you to perform your daily tasks or work if you have been in an accident.

Your physiotherapist in Winnipeg can treat you, no matter the nature of your injury. No matter if you are recovering from surgery, or have a broken muscle, Winnipeg physiotherapy can help you get back your mobility and flexibility.

Because it's all about you, your session will be special because each one is unique. In general, here's what happens:

  • We learn about your medical history
  • Your condition will be assessed and diagnosed by us
  • A treatment plan is created that outlines both the issues found during the assessment as well as the goals for treatment.
  • Joint mobilization/manipulation and manual therapy techniques, IMS, and/or electrical modalities in combination with a course of exercises will be used as indicated and acceptable to you
  • Prescribed assistive devices, bracing, exercise equipment, or other orthopedic supplies if needed.

What is the best time to see a physiotherapist?

A physiotherapist can help you if your mobility and ability to function has changed. This could be caused by a recent injury or surgery. But physiotherapists can also help prevent injury. So instead of waiting for discomfort to change into a loss of function, you would benefit from the help of a skilled and certified manipulative physiotherapist.

What Is Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a primary health care profession which helps people to restore, maintain and maximize their strength and physical function and improve their overall well-being. They learn how to pinpoint the root cause of an injury and/or the multiple factors involved in movement dysfunction and chronic pain.

Conditions that Require Winnipeg Physiotherapy

If you suffer from any of these injuries, physiotherapy Winnipeg will be beneficial to your recovery.

  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
  • Groin or abdominal complex strain
  • Hip or groin pain
  • Ligament sprains and tears
  • Overuse injuries
  • Tears and muscle strains
  • Shin splints
  • Shoulder problems
  • Tennis/golfer's elbow

Physiotherapy can also help you recover after surgery.
